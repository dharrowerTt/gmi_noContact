Creation and Operation of the Canadian Emissions Reduction Innovation Consortium

The oil and gas industry currently lacks streamlined support for critically needed, novel, high performance, and cost-effective technologies to address the global emissions reduction challenge. Petroleum Technology Alliance Canada (PTAC) created the Canadian Emissions Reductions Innovation Consortium (CanERIC) to respond to this industry gap. CanERIC is a network of emissions reduction test facilities with a vision to perform the following:

CMC Research Institute testing tool

  • Strongly encourage national integration and collaboration
  • Cover a wide spectrum of methane detection, mitigation, quantification, conservation, conversion, flaring, and Short Lived Climate Pollutants (SLCP) for the entire upstream, midstream, and downstream hydrocarbon industry and all hydrocarbon types, including conventional and unconventional oil and natural gas (oil sands, heavy oil, tight oil, shale gas)
  • Avoid duplication and close technology and field testing gaps;
  • Provide a one-window access to a full range of specialized facilities to Canadian innovators
  • Host an open information sharing platform
  • Build capacity and skills in highly qualified personnel (HQP)
  • Maintain Canadian global innovation leadership

From coast-to-coast, CanERIC supports producers, technology providers, academia, regulators, and policy makers and ensures equitable opportunities to all—including women, indigenous communities, immigrants, and remote/small communities. By embracing collaboration, CanERIC provides a complete suite of facilities, such as labs, piloting spaces, mobile instrumentation units, and commercial production sites, that respond to the innovation needs of technology readiness level (TRL) 5-9 technologies related to upstream detection, quantification, mitigation, conservation, conversion, and flaring of methane.

One Window Access for Researchers and Innovators

The innovation journey is complex and expensive for new technologies that reduce methane and SLCP emissions in the oil and gas industry. A wide range of technical requirements must be met in diverse settings, while providing low-cost solutions that comply with regulations. Innovators struggle to gain access to all of the required test facilities that allow them to address these complexities. CanERIC streamlines this journey for researchers and innovators by providing simplified access to all facility types along the innovation pathway, from labs to field production sites. In addition, CanERIC helps to ensure that technology providers and researchers are informed about the specific challenges faced by producers and regulators. This information fosters the development of market-driven technologies that also satisfy regulatory requirements.

Open Information Sharing Platform

Innovation is currently hampered and delayed by barriers to information; therefore, CanERIC will enable a centralized, national-level hub for information collection and dissemination. Stakeholders, particularly researchers and technology developers, have indicated that easier and faster access to highly relevant information would accelerate their workflow and reduce their cost by more accurately targeting market requirements.

CanERIC Reduction Targets

  • Increase sector methane emissions reduction capacity from 30 percent to 45 percent by 2022
  • Achieve 90 percent methane reduction technology capacity by 2035
  • Help oil and gas producers meet:
    • 472 MMTCO2e/year by 2025*
    • 945 MMTCO2e/year by 2035*
*assuming methane is 25 times more potent than CO2

Initiatives in Methane Detection, Quantification, and Reporting

The Petroleum Technology Alliance Canada (PTAC) Alberta Upstream Petroleum Research Program (AUPRF) is a unique environmental, peer-reviewed applied research program that manages a methane detection and quantification program in Canada. AUPRF participants include more than 400 producers, Alberta and British Columbia regulators, policy makers, Canadian universities, and other researchers and technology providers. Funding for AUPRF is provided through the Alberta Energy Regulator and voluntary contributions from producers, as well as funding leveraged from other sources.

AMFC 1.0 Kick-off team picture at Rocky Mountain House, Alberta
PTAC is founded on two pillars: innovation and collaboration. PTAC’s consortia developed the collective technology capacity to help Canada’s oil & gas sector reduce overall methane emissions by more than 30 percent in the last decade. By 2021, PTAC aims to increase technology capacity to meet Canada’s oil & gas industry target for reducing methane emissions by 45 percent by 2025.

Methane Emissions Reduction Network

Petroleum Technology Alliance Canada (PTAC) is building an innovation ecosystem to facilitate the development of methane emission detection and reduction for oil and gas operations.

For more information about MERN, including focus areas, resources, and news, visit the Methane Hub website at methanehub.ptac.org.
Given the high priority focus on methane reductions initiatives within the Canadian oil and gas industry, PTAC, in collaboration with their industry partners, developed the Methane Emissions Reduction Network (MERN). MERN unlocks opportunities for innovators, academic researchers, and subject matter experts (SME) to connect with and learn from end users, as well as local and international peers. In developing MERN, PTAC addressed a critical need to dramatically reduce methane and other greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions through industry awareness and collaboration, while accelerating the development of decisive technologies.

MERN’s Main Objectives

  • Reduce methane and other GHG emissions and increase operational energy efficiency in the oil and gas sector by raising producer awareness of available tools and techniques that can be used immediately to facilitate positive change
  • Launch technology projects to detect, mitigate, and report methane and other GHG emissions
  • Secure funds and sites for small- and medium-sized technology providers to field test their emissions reduction technologies
  • Provide a robust and transparent process for adjudication, management, and closure of methane and other GHG emission reduction projects
  • Encourage early actions to address the Climate Leadership Plan

Highlights of MERN’s Actions to Date

  • Increased deployment and demand for existing methane emissions reduction technologies, products, and services from Canadian service and supply companies (MERN’s education activities have the potential to increase current penetration from less than 10 percent to 30 percent in the near term)
  • Development, demonstration, and commercialization of new Canadian methane emissions reduction products and services to address some of the more difficult methane emission reduction challenges and increase market penetration to 45 percent by 2025

PTAC Methane Emissions Reduction Forum in Banff, Alberta

MERN’s Planned Actions

  • Provide uniform, neutral, and complete communications to all stakeholders.
  • Communicate educational and knowledgeable mobilization opportunities through media articles, web posts, webinars, workshops, and other MERN events, including the annual Methane Emissions Reduction Forum
  • Align the current fledging community of practices into a common understanding of research needs, technology gaps, scientific opportunities, and promising developments
  • Launch eminently relevant and market-driven applied research and technology development projects, informed by respected scientific advisors, experienced end users, and prospective customers
  • Recognize excellence through annual student and SME innovation awards

Building Technology Capacity to Reduce Methane Emissions at Lower Costs

Petroleum Technology Alliance Canada (PTAC) acts as a neutral facilitator and manager for methane mitigation projects and contributes up to 15 percent of funding to help advance projects that reduce methane emissions. By forming numerous consortia and securing sites and funding, PTAC helps technology providers develop, commercialize, deploy, and successfully market their technologies.

Examples

PTAC has been a forum for hundreds of technology providers, producers, academia, regulators, government organizations, and investors to collaborate and improve cost-effective new technologies to reduce oil and gas sector methane emissions.
