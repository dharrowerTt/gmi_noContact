TEREGA: An Involved Actor in Clean Energy Transition 

TEREGA, a French transmission system operator, owns more than 5,000 km of natural gas grid in the southwest of France and 2 underground gas storage facilities that represent 25 percent of French gas storage capacity.

TEREGA is taking action to reduce its global environmental footprint by:

  • Preserving the biodiversity with their “Avoid - Reduce - Compensate” approach
  • Signing the Act4NAture commitment
  • Promoting biodiversity as a committed actor of the Club of Linear Infrastructures for Biodiversity
  • Managing compensation spaces under the ITTECOP research program through strong commitments to the development of biomethane within the New Aquitaine Region
  • Increasing their research partnership to better support advances in biomethane and hydrogen gas utilization and to offer reliable energy transition infrastructure

As part of their BILAN ENVIRONMENTAL POSITIF (BE Positif) program, TEREGA has established voluntary and ambitious objectives to achieve a neutral environmental footprint by 2020 and a positive footprint by 2050.

An Action Plan to Reduce Emissions

TEREGA has identified the following specific actions to monitor, inventory, avoid, and reduce methane emissions:

  • Use cartography and develop a quantification model for fugitive emissions—methane emissions are approximately one third of TEREGA’s greenhouse gas balance
  • Create an internal decision-making tool to optimize and reduce energy consumption of compressors used for gas transmission
  • Minimize the impacts of maintenance operations to grid pressure
  • Reduce fatal emissions and the associated environmental impacts by investing in mobile recompression units and burning devices to prevent the release of several thousand tons of methane into the atmosphere per year
