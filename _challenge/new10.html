Ingevity Corporation, Adsorbed Natural Gas (ANG) for Flare Gas Recovery and Utilization

The GTI project is based at GTI's headquarters in Des Plaines, IL. The company name and country involved for the large field ANG trials cannot be disclosed due to confidentiality agreement provisions.

Multilateral. See responses above.

Partners: DNV GL (United Kingdom) completed a comprehensive, full-scale demonstration of ANG technology in 2013 which validated that "ANG technology gives the greatest carbon (GHG) footprint reduction compared to recompression and flaring." The Gas Technology Institute (IL, USA) has initiated an ANG evaulation project this year funded by its Operations Technology Development (OTD) group of leading natural gas utilities. The GTI project scope describes "Bulk ANG storage combined with a gas compressor, gas conditioning, and/or a pressure regulating station could provide an economically attractive alternative to installation of new pipe to 1) meet the peak demands of a system, 2) provide emergency backup for increased resiliency, or 3) offer a means for eliminating venting of gas when taking gas mains out of service for replacement or repairs." A major European oil & gas producer is currently conducting a field test of ANG recovery and storage involving over 10 metric tons of activated carbon to subsequently fuel an electric generator supplying electricity to the grid.

Measuring Effectiveness

Success will be measured by ANG technology's ability to reduce cost and/or carbon emissions relative to alternative methods, such as compressed natural gas (CNG).

Expected Outcomes

GTI will provide reports to its OTD group of natural gas utilities and participating adsorbent suppliers (like Ingevity).

Methane Reductions

The DNV GL study quantified and compared the carbon footprint of venting (6,574 kg CO2e, 100%) to flaring (1,504 kg CO2e, 22.9%) to CNG (44 kg CO2e, 0.7%) to ANG (3 kg CO2e, 0.1%).
