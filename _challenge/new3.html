Saskatchewan Research Council Centre for the Demonstration of Emissions Reductions (CeDER)

The focus is primarily in the Western Canadian Sedimentary Basin. This includes cross-provincial project work and initiatives encompassing British Columbia, Alberta and Manitoba as well as SRC’s home province of Saskatchewan.

Domestic

Partners: We work with several partners including Petroleum Technology Alliance Canada (PTAC), the Methane Emissions Reduction Network (MERN) and the Methane Emissions Leadership Alliance (MELA) to promote the services offered by CeDER and identify opportunities to reduce methane emissions.

Measuring Effectiveness

We track how many technologies have been successfully deployed in the field that have gone through the testing and verification with CeDER.

Expected Outcomes

We track the increase in the number of companies using the services year over year. This includes new technology companies and upstream operators.

The expected emissions reductions should be significant. However, the size of the reductions is subject to the identification and wide-scale field deployment of technologies that have been determined to have economic and technical viability.
