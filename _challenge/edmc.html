East Delhi Municipal Corporation (EDMC) 

The East Delhi Municipal Corporation is improving waste management practices and reducing methane emissions in New Delhi, India. In 2016, EDMC partnered with the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency and the Climate and Clean Air Coalition’s (CCAC) Waste Initiative to prepare a workplan to identify the actions needed to mitigate short-lived climate pollutants (SLCP) from the municipal solid waste sector. In addition to improving waste management practices, the workplan included actions to reduce SLCP emissions.

The workplan was developed using CCAC’s Waste Initiative’s Solid Waste Emissions Estimation Tool (SWEET) to analyze the environmental benefits of a planned waste treatment facility.

Actions Taken in Support of SLCP Workplan 

Since development of the SLCP workplan, EDMC has reduced climate impacts by installing waste processing facilities and is focusing efforts on reducing the amount of waste to landfills.

EDMC has:

  • Installed 10 decentralized compost units
  • Installed two decentralized bio-methanation units
  • Deployed 16 light motor vehicles, which utilize compressed natural gas fuel
  • Promoted utilization of electrical and cleaner fuel in automobiles
