GEMMA Real-Time Emissions Monitoring

Questor’s Gas Emissions & Methane Monitoring Analytics (GEMMA) is a digital data platform that provides 24/7 performance monitoring of Questor’s geographically dispersed field devices. Initial site evaluations establish a baseline for the "before" emissions scenario to assess results after emission-reducing activities and technologies are applied. Field sensors that are connected as an Internet of Things (IoT) automatically upload emissions data to a secure cloud server, which relays the uploaded emissions to a central Emissions Excellence Center (EEC) located in Calgary, Canada. These data provide a basis for comparing before and after results.

Once uploaded to the EEC, Questor manages the data on a blockchain-based platform that stores data in a transparent and highly auditable format that can be viewed by any interested (and authorized) parties. Clients receive automated emission reduction reports, which facilitate information sharing with regulators, investors, and other third-party groups. The quantification, generation, verification, and exchange of emissions reductions credits are also automated.


Source: Questor Technologies
The data system builds upon Questor's patented world leading waste gas Thermal Oxidizer systems with the ability to consolidate sensor data from a multitude of sources, including satellites, drones, and hand-held and fixed detectors to paint a complete picture of site emissions.

GEMMA creates economic and process efficiencies by allowing continuous remote monitoring of geographically dispersed field installations and drastically reducing on-site maintenance requirements. Ongoing data analysis using artificial intelligence instantly identifies and signals anomalous performance, such as equipment failure or environmental alarm conditions, enabling real-time response and minimizing methane emissions.

Thermal Oxidizers

Questor is tackling methane at the source by using their thermal oxidizers in Colorado and North Dakota.


Source: Questor Technologies

Through this process:

  • Questor reduces 28 tons of CO2e for every ton of methane combusted
  • Volatile organic compounds are oxidized to CO2 and water vapor–significantly reducing ground-level ozone formation

Emission Reduction Credits

In Mexico, Questor is tracking performance data and participating in greenhouse gas economics through verification of emissions reductions credits.


Source: Questor Technologies

From the Mexico site:

  • Total combined gas flow rate is 145 thousand cubic feet per hour
  • Greenhouse gas intensity from venting is 1,404 tons of CO2e per day
  • Greenhouse gas intensity of the outlet gas is 193 tons of CO2e per day
  • Greenhouse gas reduction using Questor thermal oxidizers is 1,208 tons of CO2e per day

This results in emission reduction credits for the site of approximately $11,023,000 per year (1,208 tons CO2e/day * 365 days/year * $25/ton CO2e).
