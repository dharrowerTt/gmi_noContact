Reciclo Orgánicos: A Paradigm Shift in the Management of Organic Residues in Chile 

Reciclo Orgánicos is a campaign to promote strategies and techniques to change the way that organic waste is managed throughout Chile. Arcadis Canada is managing the program in concert with the Ministry of Environment Chile. Environment and Climate Change Canada (ECCC) provides the funding as part of the Canada-Chile Agreement on Environmental Cooperation, which promotes environmental protection and sustainable development in both countries.

A main objective of Reciclo Orgánicos is to divert organic waste from uncontrolled landfills to reduce the emission of greenhouse gases that are generated naturally when organic solid waste decomposes. By diverting the waste, the organic matter can be used productively to create compost and fertilizer for agriculture or to generate biogas that can be used as a renewable energy source to generate electricity. Actions being taken through the Reciclo Orgánicos program protect the soil, water bodies, and air quality in Chile and are helping the Chilean government meet its nationally determined contributions (NDCs) under the Paris Agreement.

Community Engagement

Another key aspect of the Reciclo Orgánicos program is to educate the public about the benefits of separating organic wastes. The #YoReciclOrganicos campaign, which is active on Facebook, Twitter, YouTube, and Instagram, encourages the public to learn more and become involved in the program.

Educational Materials

Members of the public can download several types of educational materials from the Reciclo Orgánicos website, including:

  • Fact sheets about climate change, greenhouse gases, types of waste, and waste management and disposal
  • A virtual library
  • Infographics
  • Booklets that describe sustainable practices
