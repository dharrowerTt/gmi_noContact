The Global Methane Challenge is open to Global Methane Initiative (GMI) Partner Countries and Project Network members. GMI focuses on reducing methane emissions from several key sectors: oil and gas systems, coal mines, and biogas (agriculture, municipal solid waste, and wastewater). Learn more about how to join GMI.

The Challenge 

The Global Methane Challenge is your chance to tell the world what you are doing to reduce methane emissions. Whether you are ready to make a new commitment or want to highlight an ongoing effort, we want to showcase it here.

The goal of the Global Methane Challenge is to encourage GMI Partner Countries and Project Network members to take ambitious action to reduce methane and to showcase the policies and technologies they are using to reduce emissions around the world. The Challenge will culminate in a capstone event in 2020, where we will celebrate our collective accomplishments.

Here are some ideas for how you can participate:

  • Monitor methane emissions and create an emissions inventory.

  • Develop an action plan for reducing emissions in one or more sectors.

  • Provide technical or financial support to a methane mitigation project.

  • Educate the public about methane emissions and abatement opportunities.

  • Showcase a methane mitigation project or technology.
