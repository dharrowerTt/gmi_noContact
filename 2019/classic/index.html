Methane is a potent greenhouse gas that traps 28 times more heat in the atmosphere than carbon dioxide, resulting in a stronger influence on warming during methane’s 12-year atmospheric life time. Reducing methane emissions can have significant climate change benefits in the near term, and capturing and using methane also offers opportunities to generate new sources of clean energy.

Global Methane Initiative (GMI) participants reduce barriers to the recovery and use of methane as a clean energy source across three key sectors. Special emphasis is given to engaging stakeholders, including governments, financial institutions, project developers, technology providers, and local communities. The GMI also shares technical tools and resources, provides training and capacity building, and conducts technology demonstrations.

GMI Partners and Particpants

  • 45 Partner Countries
  • Project Network that reaches more than 1,200 members, including private companies, financial institutions, universities, and other organizations
  • Key alliances the United Nations Economic Commission for Europe (UNECE) and the Climate and Clean Air Coalition (CCAC)

Making a Difference Since 2004

  • Achieved methane reductions of 10.7 million metric tons
  • Trained more than 15,000 people in methane mitigation
  • Conducted more than 600 resource assessments, feasibility studies, site visits, and site tours
